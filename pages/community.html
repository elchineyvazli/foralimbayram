COMMUNITY CENTER FOR THE HORACE MANN SCHOOL OF THE DEAF AND HARD HEARING

Reflecting on my experience designing a special building for deaf and hard-of-hearing people, I found it to be an insightful and fulfilling experience, mainly when presenting my work to a member of the deaf community during our presentation day. As an architect, it is essential to consider multiple factors and approach projects from various angles, and this project challenged me to think creatively and critically in many ways.The building was thoughtfully designed and separated into three floors. The first and largest floor serves as the entry point, featuring two classrooms, restrooms, a computer lab, a kitchen and cafeteria, private areas, and a garden surrounded by a ramp. The second floor, though containing the fewest rooms, is the longest part of the building and includes an art room, a multipurpose area, and a stunning yoga room. The yoga room is a visual centerpiece connected to the multipurpose area via a glass wall entrance and boasts the most natural lighting in the building. The third section is the highest part of the building, is accessible and houses the office rooms. Overall, the design is innovative and tailored specifically to the needs of the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. I am proud to have worked on this project and contributed to creating a building that fosters inclusivity and accessibility.

FRAGMENT MODEL

The 1"=4'0" scale section fragment model of the Horace Mann School of the Deaf and Hard Hearing provides a detailed visual representation of the building's design, showcasing the careful consideration given to accessibility and inclusivity for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. The model accurately depicts the garden, classroom, staircases, multipurpose area, and yoga room, providing insight into the building's layout and the relationships between different areas.
